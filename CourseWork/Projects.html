My Journey in Building Digital Experiences: From Data to Design

Over the past few years, I’ve been exploring the intersection of code, creativity, and user experience — turning ideas into functional, elegant digital solutions. My portfolio showcases a range of projects that reflect my growth as a web developer and technology enthusiast, from front-end design to data-driven analysis.

From Code Logic to User Flow: The Developer Side

My journey began with a strong foundation in Python, where I worked on scenario-based problems involving input validation, data visualization, and file operations. One of my early projects analyzed vehicle traffic data, featuring functions to validate user inputs, count nighttime vehicle activity, and visualize hourly traffic patterns using histograms enhanced with average markers. These experiences taught me how data accuracy and usability go hand-in-hand even the smallest validation check can dramatically improve user trust and application performance.

United Motors Vehicle Sales Dashboard

One of my proudest projects was creating a Vehicle Sales Dashboard for United Motors Sri Lanka, built using Access, MySQL and PowerBI. The goal was to analyze vehicle stock levels, pre-delivery inspections (PDI), and sales performance using real data from RMV and internal inventory sources. I designed the system to provide managers with interactive views, allowing them to filter vehicles by model, status, and sales region helping them make data-driven decisions quickly and efficiently.

Key Features Included:

  • Interactive charts showing monthly and yearly sales trends.
  • Automated summaries of available vs. sold units by model.
  • Linked Access database for structured vehicle information.
  • Dynamic formulas and conditional formatting for visual insights.

    • Through this project, I learned the importance of data relationships, query optimization, and presentation clarity ensuring that complex datasets become actionable intelligence for business users.

Sustainafy: Building for a Better Tomorrow

As part of my Web Design and Development coursework at the University of Westminster, my team and I developed Sustainafy, a website dedicated to Sustainable Cities and Communities, inspired by one of the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). We took design inspiration from Samsung’s Galaxy S25 Ultra website — focusing on simplicity, responsiveness, and visual storytelling. My contribution centered on front-end development, where I refined layouts using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, creating an intuitive and elegant interface. This project solidified my love for clean, purposeful design that communicates as effectively as it functions.

Exploring the Future of Security with Quantum Computing

In my research report titled “What Impact Might Quantum Computing Have on Computer Security?”, I explored how upcoming technologies may disrupt current encryption systems.

  • Breaking Classical Encryption through quantum algorithms
  • Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) as the new standard
  • Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) for secure communication
  • Emerging Security Paradigms adapting to quantum threats

Designing the Future: Bridging Code and Creativity

Beyond coding, I’ve been developing my UI/UX design skills through Figma, working on dashboard layouts and learning how to translate design concepts into code. Understanding the code-to-design workflow has helped me create interfaces that are both visually consistent and technically optimized.
